Tic-Tac-Toe Championship Edition

This is a full-stack game application of tic-tac-toe, users register and play a game against the computer. A leaderboard tracks the top 10 players based on wins and displays them in real-time. This app's target audience is for users that like to play simple games to pass the time. My inspiration for building this app comes from my lifelong passion to build a game from scratch, this includes the style theme of an old monochromatic monitor predominantly seen during the 1980s, the era I was introduced to video games.

Quiz App

A multiple-choice quiz app, that tracks a user's answers and displays an animation on the selection of correct or incorrect answers. A final score is displayed at the end with a unique animation based on the total correct answers. This app's target audience is for pop culture fans of the late 90s specifically sci-fi fans of the matrix movie which is also my inspiration for the theme. I was also inspired by characters in the movie being programmers which as a young adult seeded the idea that I could one day become a programmer myself.

Marvel Comics App

A Marvel Comics App that utilizes the Marvel API, users enter a date range to retrieve a random selection of marvel comics published within the user's dates. Up to 10 comics can be retrieved per user submission. The target audience for this app was for comic book fans of Marvel or comics in general. My inspiration for building this app came from me being able to remember comics based on their compelling covers, but not necessarily their storylines or other details. So we created in essence a type of search with only a limited input of a date range.

About Me

I am a full-stack developer and proud graduate of Thinkful Bootcamp for software engineering. My passion for coding stems from my experiences in tinkering with all aspects of technology from my childhood to the present day! Whether fixing VHS players as a kid, to repairing circuit boards as a young adult or programming, I have always been enthusiastic about breaking down technology to understand it and figuring out how to fix it or improve it. Solving problems is what continues to motivate me whether that be a simple issue or something complex like learning different programming languages.


Another motivator for me is working with teams small or large. I have found that as I grow in every career, team-work has played a big role in growing as an individual. Bouncing ideas off of teammates opens the door to different approaches to problem-solving, that as a lone wolf you may miss out on.
